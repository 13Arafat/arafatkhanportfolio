Run away with the Cirus

This concept is based on looking back in time with a sense of curiosity and nostalgia,honoring historical designs from 20th-century circus costumes and merging them with modern tastes to create a futuristic vision.Inspired by both the past and the future, the collection incorporates elements from the British Raj period, focusing on circus attire details and juxtaposing them with contemporary elements to achieve a hyper-digital style.Run Away with the Circus is an A/W 23 collection that appeals to the next generation of 21+ consumers who are attracted to high-end fashion,using the past as a reference point to redefine the future within a digital world.

Ikat artisan

Ikat is a textile pattern created by dyeing threads before the fabric is woven, traditionally hand woven in Southeast Asia and South America and now reimagined as a fashion-forward print on international runways. While some designers push Ikat to the extreme, others explore more subtle interpretations through panels and floral blends. I followed this softer approach for this inspiration board, mixing Ikat prints with colorful exotic blooms to create a worldly, chic feel that translates easily into summer, and can be styled with mixed prints, bold colors, and tribal-inspired jewelry for an effortlessly on-trend look.

Dhaka Dreams

In response to the pandemic situation, historical art from Bangladesh has been chosen as a key source of inspiration, aligning with current fashion trends. This heritage reflects craftsmanship, creativity, a hand-crafted sensibility, and a strong connection to history through harmonious color palettes. Alongside this, the theme of Modern Art of Bangladesh reinterprets these traditional values in a contemporary context, expressing creativity, artistry, history, and a hand-created feel through a modern visual language relevant to today’s world.

waste or wonder

Waste or Wonder is an A/W 22 menswear knitwear collection focused on creating sustainable solutions through innovative knit constructions with a high-fashion approach. Concerned with the fashion industry’s overconsumption of fabric, the collection is inspired by recycling offcuts, minimizing material waste, and producing conceptual, sustainable garments for the next generation. Witnessing the industry’s environmental impact was shocking and made me reflect on my role as a designer. This motivated me to create zero-waste pieces a challenging but achievable goal demonstrating that fashion can be both creative and environmentally responsible, proving sustainability and style can coexist.
